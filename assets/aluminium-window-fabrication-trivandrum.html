The Fabrication Process Explained

Behind every stylish aluminium window is a carefully planned fabrication process. Here's a simplified breakdown of how it works:

01

Design and Measurement

The process begins with accurate measurements and design planning based on the client's requirements. This ensures the windows fit perfectly and align with the overall architectural design.

02

Cutting and Shaping

Aluminium profiles are cut into precise lengths and shapes using advanced machinery. This stage demands accuracy to guarantee smooth assembly and a flawless final product.

03

Assembly and Welding

The cut profiles are assembled into frames using specialized welding or joining techniques. At this stage, openings for locks, handles, and glass panels are integrated into the frame design.

04

Surface Finishing

To improve durability and aesthetics, the aluminium frames are finished with powder coating or anodizing. This not only adds colour but also protects the frame from corrosion and wear.

05

Glass Installation

Glass panels are carefully fitted into the fabricated frames. Options include clear, tinted, frosted, or double-glazed glass for added functionality and style.

06

Final Inspection

The finished windows are inspected for quality, strength, and appearance before installation. This ensures every product meets high standards of performance and design.
